Jiawei Gu

I am a joint postdoctoral fellow at the Tsinghua Shenzhen International Graduate School, advised by Prof. Yujiu Yang, and at Great Bay University, advised by Prof. Ziyue Qiao. I received my Ph.D. in Computer Science from Nanjing University of Science and Technology in 2026, under the supervision of Prof. Zechao Li.

My research lies at the intersection of deep learning and applied mathematics, with a focus on the Structural Reliability of Neural Representations: a unified study of when, why, and how modern deep models silently fail, conducted from the inside. The deployment failures that matter most today, including overconfident wrong predictions, hidden bias, and breakdown under low precision, are not random noise. They leave identifiable signatures in the geometry, spectrum, and numerics of what a model has learned, and these signatures admit principled diagnosis and lightweight correction.

My work is organized around three perspectives:

  • Geometry: the shape of feature distributions, the flow of local gradients, and the dynamics of hidden-state trajectories reveal where a prediction is trustworthy and where it has drifted off the manifold.
  • Spectrum: eigenvalues, Laplacians, and covariance structure expose anomalies, ill-conditioning, and collapse modes that scalar accuracy alone cannot detect.
  • Numerics: finite-precision arithmetic, conditioning, and compilation decide whether the model in theory still matches the model in production.
A defining choice across my work is to keep every correction post-hoc, lightweight, and theoretically grounded. These are methods that require no retraining, integrate cleanly into existing pipelines, and come with a clear mathematical account of why they work. I aim for tools that real systems can adopt today, and that science can falsify tomorrow.
